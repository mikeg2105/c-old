PRESSURE BOUNDARY CONDITIONS

In order to model problems where the inlet and exit pressures rather than the velocity values are known, pressure boundary conditions can be used. Examples include problems involving natural convection and problems where multiple exits to the domain exist and the division of flow between the outlets are determined by the pressure difference between them. Pressures are specified relative to the operating pressure and are in terms of Pascals. Turbulence Intensity can not be specified on pressure boundaries. Therefore even if the turbulence is switched on, user will not be asked to input turbulence values at the inlet boundaries. One way of overcoming this restriction will be to patch-in turbulence dissapation-rate and energy at the inlet cells, via the expert-patch menu.
